Development Handin

Introduction

The goal of this Development project was to create a list of items similar to an online store or list maker with sorting, filtering, and an aggregator like a cart or favorites list.

I did a competitive analysis on three companies who all had features that I thought about either implementing or improving on in my own project.

I decided to create a Hike To-Do List Maker since it combines my large passion of the outdoors with the objectives of the project.

Competitive Analysis

I chose Spotify, Kuhl, and All Trails to do my competitive analysis on.

I found that AllTrails definitely had a very appealing filtering system, with a map on the right side of the screen to show exactly where each hike on my favorites list was. It also had a plethora of filtering options. Not being able to sort the hikes was an unfortunate downside, as being able to sort by longest to shortest or highest to lowest would be nice.

Kuhl had a nice visual design, but it was lacking many features that one would want out of a shopping cart, as there was no way to filter OR sort the items. However, navigating the items/removing items was a very easy task and the simplified and streamlined interface was nice to use.

Finally, Spotify does a lot right since they have an extremely large team behind them. They include a lot of sorting options and even make it so you can create custom orders for songs. The one thing they were missing was any sort of filtering system, whether it be black or whitelisting. I would like to see that in the future from Spotify.

For my interface, I wanted to have a system that sorts, filters, and removes mountains/hikes like Alltrails does, but I would like to focus on more information about the hikes themselves and less on the overall map of all the hikes, as it can seem crowded and hard to read. I want to create a to-do list of hikes on the side and in the middle would be all of the available hikes, with the ability to add or remove from the to-do list, sort by length or height (both high to low and low to high), filter by difficulty or state, or both, clear to-do list, and reset filters/sorts

Development Process

I implemented the filtering and sorting on the hike cards and they can compound on each other, for example, a user can sort from highest to lowest height, as well as only hikes from New Hampshire rated Hard.

When the user adds a hike to the to-do list, it will remove it from the main selection area and place it in the to-do list. The card will now show a remove button instead of an add button. This is because I don't believe it makes sense to add a specific mountain more than once.
